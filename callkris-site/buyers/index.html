Our Buying Advantage

Our buying process

Stage 1

Getting to know you

Every relationship starts with a conversation about your home-buying goals, preferences and budget. We want to understand your needs, give you an overview of the buying process, and answer every question — making things as easy and stress-free as possible.

Stage 2

Getting pre-approved

Before your home search begins, we'll help you get pre-approved for a mortgage. We can recommend reputable lenders and guide you through the process so we find the right home for your budget.

Stage 3

Finding the perfect home

Now the fun begins. We scour the market for properties that meet your needs and budget, send regular updates on new listings, and coordinate showings around your schedule — so you never miss out on your perfect home.

Stage 4

Viewing the homes

When we find a property you're interested in, we'll schedule a viewing and accompany you with expert guidance. We help you assess the property's features and condition and provide insights into the local market so you can make an informed decision.

Stage 5

Putting in the offer

When you love a property, we've got your back. We help draft and submit the offer, advise on the appropriate price and contingencies, and negotiate on your behalf if there are any counteroffers.

Stage 6

Negotiating the offer

If the seller accepts, we negotiate the terms of the sale to make sure you get the best possible outcome — with expert advice on the strength of the offer and any potential risks or benefits.

Stage 7

Closing on your home

Once the offer is accepted, we close the deal — coordinating with lawyers, notaries and lenders to ensure a smooth transaction. We review all closing documents with you and explain any fees or charges.

Stage 8

Post-purchase aftercare

After the sale, we follow up to address any outstanding issues, answer your questions, and provide ongoing support as you settle into your new home. We want to make sure you feel right at home.

Home Buyer's FAQ

Most frequently asked questions

How much can I afford to spend on a house?

We recommend speaking with a lender to determine your pre-approved amount. They'll evaluate your financial situation and provide a price range you can comfortably afford.

How do I get pre-approved for a mortgage?

We can recommend a few reputable lenders to choose from. They'll review your financial information and provide a pre-approval letter showing how much you can afford to borrow.

What should I look for when viewing a property?

Consider the overall condition of the property, the neighbourhood, and any potential issues that might affect your future ownership experience. We provide expert guidance during every viewing to help you assess a property's features and condition.

How do I make an offer on a property?

We help you draft and submit an offer to the seller's agent, advise on the appropriate offer price and contingencies, and negotiate on your behalf to secure the best terms possible.

How long does it take to close on a house?

The closing process varies, but typically takes 30–45 days. We help facilitate the process, coordinating with lawyers, notaries, lenders and brokerages to ensure a smooth transaction.

What is a home inspection, and should I get one?

A home inspection is an assessment of the property's condition. We highly recommend getting one so you have an expert opinion on any potential issues before you buy.

What is a subject, and what should be included in my offer?

A subject is a condition that must be waived or fulfilled before the deal goes firm. We'll help you determine which subjects to include in your offer, such as a home inspection or financing approval.

How do I negotiate with the seller?

With 250+ homes sold and credentials as an Accredited Real Estate Negotiator, we work together to ensure you're getting the best deal possible.

What are the closing costs associated with buying a house?

Closing costs vary and typically include applicable taxes, conveyance fees, and — while not a direct closing cost — an inspection. As a guide: Property Transfer Tax roughly 2%, GST 5% if applicable, conveyance fees around $1,250, and inspection $500–$750.

Do I pay Realtor fees when buying a home?

While commissions are negotiable in Canada, the seller most often offers a commission to be paid to the buyer's agent for bringing the buyer and processing the transaction. Generally speaking, you will not pay for your agent's fee.
